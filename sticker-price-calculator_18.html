Per-share figures where available. FCF/share is reconstructed from FCF margin × sales/share. These are past rates, not promises — they tell you what growth has actually looked like, so your forward assumption isn't pulled from thin air.

How this works. Project today's earnings forward at your growth rate, turn that into a future share price using a future P/E, then discount it back to today at the return you demand. That discounted figure is the sticker price — what the stock is worth to you now. The tag then shows how today's price compares: a discount means the market is offering it below fair value, a premium means above. The bigger the discount, the more room for error in your assumptions.

Where to find the numbers. EPS and price come from any quote page or the auto-fill button. Growth is your own estimate — analysts' 5-yr forecasts and the company's past EPS growth are reasonable anchors, but the figure is a judgment call. A common rule of thumb for future P/E is twice the growth rate, capped at the historical P/E.

This is an educational tool, not investment advice. Every output depends entirely on assumptions you supply; small changes in growth or P/E move the result a lot. Do your own research.
